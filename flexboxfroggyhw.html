And now for the actual answer you were looking for...

For starters, right now the frogs are displayed in a row, but to get them onto the lily pads, we're going to need to switch a column layout. We want the red and yellow frogs to swap, so it looks like we'll be needing a "column-reverse." We want to assign a wrap so that the frogs retain the correct size. "Wrap-reverse" will make sure the yellow and red frogs stay on the correct sides. After these two moves, the green and red frogs are home, but the yellow frogs are stuck in the pond. We need to move the yellow frogs up and over. Adding "justify-content:center;" will achieve moving the frogs up. Adding "align-content:space-between" will stretch the space between the two sets of frogs and...VOILA, they're home! Align and justify have switched from their normal functions because we are not using row, but rather, column.
